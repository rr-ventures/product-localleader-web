We help Australian trade businesses become the #1 tradie in their area.

## 2. BRAND VOICE & LANGUAGE RULES

**Voice:** Trustworthy. Relatable. Authoritative but casual. Like a tradie's smart mate who happens to know Google inside out.

**Tone rules:**
- Plain-spoken. Australian. No jargon. No fluff.
- Confident without being cocky.
- Never talks down to the audience.
- "Good at what we do, don't need to try hard to prove it."

**Language rules:**
- Always say "tradie" — never "trade professional" or "contractor."
- Outcomes before features. "Your phone rings more" before "we optimise your GBP."
- Google and Maps are fine to mention as the mechanism — but the outcome always leads the sentence.
- Australian spelling (optimise, specialise, colour).

**Never say:** "leverage", "synergy", "digital transformation", "solution", "holistic", "bespoke" (in main copy — Tier 2 can use "custom").

## 3. TARGET AUDIENCE (for copy targeting)

**Who:** Australian trade business owners — plumbers, electricians, builders, landscapers, concreters, painters, roofers, tilers. Predominantly male, 25–55. Sole traders to small teams (1–10 staff).

**Their daily reality:**
- On the tools all day. Checking their phone in the ute between jobs.
- Time-poor. Decision-fatigued. Don't want to think about marketing.
- Getting work through unpredictable word of mouth or referrals.
- Some using HiPages/Airtasker (race to the bottom on price).
- Competitors outranking them on Google. Few Google reviews.

**Their mindset:**
- Deeply sceptical of agencies — most have been burned before.
- Decide on TRUST and PROOF, not features or jargon.
- Hate complexity. Hate contracts. Hate feeling sold to.
- Competitive and territorial about their local area.

**What they want (use in copy):**
- Phone ringing without chasing.
- Booked out 4–6 weeks ahead.
- Choosing jobs, not taking whatever comes.
- Being THE go-to in their area.
- Financial predictability. More time with family.

**What they fear (use in objection handling):**
- Wasting more money on marketing that doesn't work.
- Getting locked into another contract.
- Not understanding what they're paying for.
- Looking like a mug.

**How they arrive at the website:**
- Paid ads, cold outreach (email/DM/SMS), warm referrals. They're already somewhat aware.
- The website is a VALIDATION and CONVERSION tool — not a discovery tool.

## 5. THE THREE PILLARS (website copy-ready)

Frame as OUTCOMES first, with a light "how" underneath. Google and Maps are the mechanism — the outcome always leads.

### Pillar 1: Top of Google Maps

**Headline copy:** "Show up first when locals search for your trade."

**Outcome:** When someone searches "[your trade] near me" on their phone, Google shows 3 businesses on the map. If you're not in those top 3, you're invisible. We get you there — and keep you there — so your phone rings from people ready to hire, not tyre-kickers.

**Light how:** We fully optimise your Google Business Profile, build your presence across 50+ directories, and manage your Maps ranking month to month so you stay ahead of competitors — not just in your suburb, but across your whole service area.

---

### Pillar 2: First Page of Google

**Headline copy:** "Your business on Page 1 — not your competitors, not some directory. You."

**Outcome:** When locals search for your trade in your area, your business shows up first. Organic traffic that comes to you every day without spending a cent on ads.

**Light how:** We build SEO-optimised pages on your website targeting the exact words your customers type when they're ready to book. We fix the technical issues most trade websites carry without knowing it, and expand to surrounding suburbs as your rankings grow.

---

### Pillar 3: 5-Star Review Engine

**Headline copy:** "Build a wall of 5-star reviews — without lifting a finger."

**Outcome:** More reviews means more trust means more calls. Your Google profile builds a reputation that makes you the obvious choice before anyone even picks up the phone.

**Light how:** We set up an automated system that sends review requests to your customers after every job. Happy customers go straight to Google. Customers with a concern go to a private feedback form — bad experiences never go public unfiltered. Every review gets a professional response.

## 6. PRICING & TIERS

### Tier 1 — Core Plan (the main offer)

**Display format on website:**
- ~~$2,000/month~~ → **$500/month** — EARLY BIRD OFFER
- Framing: Limited spots. Founding client pricing. Transparent about the fact this is a new offer and early clients get a significant discount.
- The $2,000 anchor is the intended long-term price. The $500 is the beta/early bird price.

**What's included (for website display):**
- Everything in the three pillars — delivered, managed, optimised monthly.
- No ad spend required on top.
- No lock-in after initial 3-month commitment.
- Month-to-month after that.

**Copy note:** Don't list granular deliverables on the pricing section. Keep it clean. The three pillars sections above do the explaining. The pricing section just confirms: "Everything above. One price. No surprises."

**ROI framing (use near pricing):**
- "What's your average job worth? One extra job from Google more than covers it."
- "No ad spend on top. Your $500/month covers everything."
- Frame weekly if helpful: ~$125/week.

## 8. CTAs & CONVERSION STRATEGY

### Primary CTA:
**"Book Your Free Strategy Call"** — Highest intent. Repeated throughout the site.

### Secondary CTA:
**"Get Your Free Local Ranking Audit"** — Lower friction. Proof before commitment. Use as an alternative entry point for more sceptical visitors.

### CTA rules (from competitor research):
- One dominant CTA repeated consistently across the site.
- Same CTA after each major section (proof, pillars, pricing).
- Secondary CTA only where it serves a different buyer state (e.g. someone not ready for a call but curious about where they rank).
- Never have more than 2 CTAs competing for attention on any single screen.

## 11. RECOMMENDED PAGE STRUCTURE

Based on competitor research and what converts in this market:

2. **Social proof strip** — Logos, review count, star ratings, or placeholder stats
3. **Problem section** — "If you're not in the top 3 on Google Maps, you're invisible." Short, punchy.
4. **Three Pillars** — Outcome-led with light "how" underneath each
5. **How it works** — Simple 3-step process (Book a call → We build your plan → Your phone starts ringing)
6. **Pricing** — ~~$2,000~~ → $500 early bird + Tier 2 authority signal
7. **Proof / case studies** — Testimonials, before/after data, results (placeholders for now)
8. **About** — Brief founder section (Reece as guide, tradie is the hero)
9. **FAQ / Objections** — See section below
10. **Final CTA block** — Repeat primary CTA with urgency framing

**Key structural insight from research:** The best competitor sites move quickly from promise → proof → contact. No heavy storytelling. No long scrolls before proof appears.

## 12. FAQ / OBJECTION HANDLING (website copy-ready)

**Q: I've been burned by marketing people before. How is this different?**
We build assets you own — your Google ranking, your Maps presence, your reviews. They don't disappear overnight. And at $500/month with no lock-in after 3 months, the risk is about as low as it gets.

**Q: How long until I see results?**
Most businesses see movement in the first 30 days. Some get calls within 2 weeks just from the Google Maps work alone. SEO compounds — month 2 is better than month 1, and it keeps building from there.

**Q: I'm too busy / don't need more work right now.**
That's the best time to build this. Word of mouth dries up. We build a pipeline that keeps your phone ringing when things slow down — so you never have to scramble.

**Q: Can't I just do this myself?**
You could. But most tradies try for a week, get busy with real jobs, and it never gets done. We do this full time, every week, and we know exactly what Google rewards.

**Q: $500 a month is a lot.**
What's your average job worth? One extra job from Google more than covers it. And there's no ad spend on top — that $500 is everything.

**Q: What's the catch with the early bird pricing?**
No catch. We're launching a new offer and giving our first clients a significant discount — $500/month instead of $2,000. You keep that rate as long as you stay. Limited spots.

**Q: Is there a lock-in contract?**
3-month initial commitment so we have time to get real results. Month-to-month after that. You stay because it's working, not because you're trapped.

**Q: What do I actually need to do?**
Almost nothing. Give us access to your Google Business Profile and website, and a list of your services and service area. We handle everything else. You'll get a monthly report showing exactly what we did and what moved.

**Q: Do you work with my type of trade?**
We work with plumbers, electricians, builders, painters, landscapers, concreters, roofers, tilers — any trade business that serves a local area and wants more local customers finding them on Google.

## 15. COMPETITIVE POSITIONING NOTES (for copy context)

**What the market does:**
- Leads with "more leads / more jobs" — outcome language.
- Heavy risk-reversal: no lock-in, free audits, guarantees.
- Heavy social proof: review walls, testimonials, badges.
- Simple, low-friction CTAs.
- Plain, tradie-coded language.

**Where the market is weak (Local Leader's opportunity):**
- Proof is broad, not forensic. Most competitors say they get results but don't show the work or the baseline.
- Pricing is usually hidden behind "book a call." Local Leader showing pricing = immediate trust advantage.
- Messaging is samey: "no BS", "more leads", "tradie specialist" are now table stakes, not differentiators.
- Few competitors position around dominance and asset-building. Most just sell lead flow.

**Local Leader doesn't need to out-design the market. It needs to out-clarify and out-proof the market.**

## 16. TECHNICAL NOTES FOR WEB BUILDERS

- This is a single-page or minimal-page site. One long-scroll homepage is fine. Additional pages only if needed (e.g. /pricing, /about, /blog in future).
- All copy should be easily editable — this is version 1 and will evolve as real proof comes in.
- Testimonials should be componentised so they can be swapped from placeholders to real ones easily.
- Design for mobile first. Most visitors will be on phones.
- Keep page speed fast. No heavy assets. Tradies on mobile data won't wait.
- All CTAs should link to a calendar booking tool (Calendly or equivalent) or a simple contact form as fallback.

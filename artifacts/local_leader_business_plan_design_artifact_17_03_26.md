## 3. THE THREE PILLARS

Framed as OUTCOMES. Google and Maps can be mentioned as the "how" — but the outcome always leads.

### Pillar 1: Top of Google Maps
When someone searches "[your trade] near me" on their phone, Google shows 3 businesses on the map. If you're not in those top 3, you're invisible. We get you there — and keep you there — so your phone rings from people ready to hire, not tyre-kickers.

### Pillar 2: First Page of Google
When locals search for your trade in your area, your business shows up first. Not your competitors. Not some directory. You. Organic traffic that comes to you every day without spending a cent on ads.

### Pillar 3: 5-Star Review Engine
We build you a system that generates real Google reviews from your happy customers — consistently, automatically. More reviews means more trust means more calls. You become the obvious choice before anyone even picks up the phone.

## 5. TARGET AUDIENCE

**Who:** Australian trade business owners — plumbers, electricians, builders, landscapers, concreters, painters, roofers, tilers. Predominantly male, 25–55. Sole traders to small teams (1–10 staff).

**Their daily reality:**
- On the tools all day. Checking their phone in the ute between jobs.
- Time-poor. Decision-fatigued. Don't want to think about marketing.
- Getting work through unpredictable word of mouth or referrals.
- Some using HiPages/Airtasker (race to the bottom on price).
- Competitors outranking them on Google. Few Google reviews.

**Their mindset:**
- Deeply sceptical of agencies — most have been burned before. Paid thousands, got nothing, couldn't understand what was being done.
- Decide on TRUST and PROOF, not features or jargon.
- Hate complexity. Hate contracts. Hate feeling like they're being sold to.
- Competitive and territorial about their local area.

**What they want (emotional):**
- Phone ringing without chasing.
- Booked out 4–6 weeks ahead.
- Choosing jobs, not taking whatever comes.
- Being THE go-to in their area.
- Financial predictability. More time with family.
- Their business feeling like a real asset, not a treadmill.

**What they fear:**
- Wasting more money on marketing that doesn't work.
- Getting locked into another contract.
- Not understanding what they're paying for.
- Looking like a mug.

**How they arrive at the website:**
- Paid ads, cold outreach (email/DM), warm referrals. They're already somewhat aware.
- The website is a VALIDATION and CONVERSION tool — not a discovery tool.

## 6. BRAND VOICE & TONE

- Trustworthy. Relatable. Authoritative but casual.
- Plain-spoken. Australian. No jargon. No fluff.
- Confident without being cocky.
- "Good at what we do, don't need to try hard to prove it."
- Talks like a tradie's smart mate who happens to know Google inside out.
- Never talks down to the audience. Never uses buzzwords to sound impressive.

**Language rules:**
- Always say "tradie" not "trade professional" or "contractor."
- Always outcomes before features. "Your phone rings more" before "we optimise your GBP."
- Google and Maps are fine to mention as the mechanism — but the outcome leads the sentence.
- Never say: "leverage", "synergy", "digital transformation", "solution."
- Australian spelling (optimise, specialise, colour).

## 7. KEY DIFFERENTIATORS

The differentiators should lean into the idea of **dominance and growth** — not just getting clients, but becoming the top player in your area. Going from someone who just takes jobs and trades time for money to building an actual business asset.

1. **Become the #1, not just "get more leads."** Every agency promises more leads. We promise you become the top tradie in your area — the one everyone calls first, the one competitors can't touch. That's a different game.

2. **No ad spend required.** Everything in the core offer is organic. No Google Ads budget on top. No Facebook spend. Your $999/month and we handle the three pillars.

3. **Build your business into an asset.** We're not renting you leads that disappear when you stop paying. We're building your Google ranking, your Maps presence, your reviews — things that compound over time and make your business worth more than just your time on the tools.

4. **Customers on autopilot.** Fill your pipeline without needing to run ads, chase leads, or rely on word of mouth alone. The system works while you're on the tools.

5. **Outgrow your competitors.** While they're relying on word of mouth and hoping the phone rings, you're systematically taking over your area. Every month you're with us, the gap between you and everyone else gets wider.

## 10. HERO VISUAL DIRECTION

The hero should visually reinforce the outcome — being #1 on Google in your area.

**Primary concept:** A Google Maps-style card showing the tradie's business at #1 position in the Map Pack with strong reviews (e.g. 4.9 stars, 100+ reviews). Should feel like looking at an actual client result, not a mockup.

**Before/after variant:** Some versions could show a before/after — before: buried on Google, few reviews, not showing in Maps. After: #1 position, hundreds of 5-star reviews, the obvious choice. (Reference: Birdeye runs this exact before/after concept in ads and it performs well long-term.)

The visual should feel like proof — data-forward, not decorative.

These are soft preferences to guide general direction. They are NOT prescriptive rules — if this artifact is paired with a separate design prompt or template system, the design prompt takes priority on visual execution. These preferences exist to give context on taste, not to override technical design decisions.

- Generally prefers left-aligned hero layouts.
- Dislikes dark/moody backgrounds, neon, gradients, startup/tech-bro vibes.

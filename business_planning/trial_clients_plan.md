## Client Overview

| # | Business | Type | Priority | Purpose | Convert to Paid? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | [Trade Client 1] | Trade (e.g. Painter) | HIGH | Case study for trades pitch | Yes — pitch $125/week |

| 4 | [Trade Client 4] | Trade / Other | MEDIUM | Practice + proof | Depends on niche fit |

### Prioritization Logic
- **HIGH priority = trades clients.** These become your case studies for pitching paid clients in the same niche. The results you get here are what you show future painters, sparkies, tilers.
- **Table tennis clubs = training reps.** No competitors means easy rankings, which is great for practicing the full GBP + Core 30 workflow without pressure. But these won't impress a trade client as case studies. Do them to get comfortable, not to build your portfolio.

## Decision Point: When To Start Pitching Paid Clients

You're ready to start selling when you have:
- [ ] At least 1 trade client with measurable before/after results
- [ ] A LeadSnap rank map showing improvement (screenshots)
- [ ] GBP insights showing increase in calls/views (screenshots)
- [ ] At least 1 review campaign showing growth (screenshots)
- [ ] Your three pillars document updated with real case study stories
- [ ] Confidence you can repeat the process (you've done it 2-3 times)

At that point, your options (in Liam's priority order):
1. Pitch remaining people in your network
2. Cold outreach to local trades
3. Paid ads for client acquisition (this is where Blueprint Training kicks in)

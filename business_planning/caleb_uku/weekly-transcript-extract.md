Rank Map Sizing and the 50% Rule





The rank map is the right size when the top competitor shows 60-80% top-three coverage. If the top player is 100% green, the map is too small. If the top player is at 20%, it's too big.



Once your business reaches about 50% of the top competitor's top-three percentage, you have enough topical relevance and should switch to producing geographic relevance content.



Geographic content targets specific neighborhoods, landmarks, intersections, and local features near the dots you want to turn green on the map.



Always sort Lead Snap results by top-three percentage, not average rank position.

Rebranding and Website Changes





Changing a domain name will cause significant rank loss, even with 301 redirects. If the business is ranking well, the recommendation is to keep the old domain and just redirect the new branded domain to it.



Even a simple WordPress theme change (same content, same images, same platform) can drop average rank position by 4-5 spots. Google does not like change.



When changing a GBP business name, there is roughly a 50/50 chance of triggering reverification. Have the client ready at their location with new signage and their phone for the video verification process.



The better the current rank map looks, the more cautious you should be about making any changes.

Restaurant SEO & Industry-Specific Tips





Restaurants are a massive untapped SEO opportunity. Most restaurant websites only have a homepage and menu, leaving topical relevance wide open.



The Core 30 approach works for restaurants. Include a plain-text menu on the site (not just a PDF) so Google and AI bots can read it. Link to the PDF as well for easy sharing.



Research what dishes and cuisine types people are searching for in your city (Reddit is a good research tool for this). Build content around those specific searches.



Use chef videos, recipes, and XML video sitemaps to build topical authority around the food types the restaurant serves.



Example: "Gumbo New Orleans" had approximately 10,000 monthly searches, and lower-rated restaurants were ranking in the top three simply because they optimized for it.

💡 Notable Q&A

Q: How should I price and manage local link building from a deep research list? A: Usually bake 1-2 link-building efforts per month into the regular retainer. Share the full research list with the client so they can pursue some opportunities themselves. Use Gemini for deep research on local link opportunities as it tends to produce better results than ChatGPT for this task.

Q: For a chiropractor with only one GBP category and 25 services, how do I structure the site? A: Put only your top 2 most important services on the GBP landing page. Create a service hub page that links to all remaining services, and link to that hub from the landing page. For insurance-related searches (e.g., "Blue Cross chiropractor"), check if the GBP results are significantly different from your main keyword. If they are, treat it as a separate entity and build content for it.

Q: For a personal injury attorney with overlapping categories, how do I re-theme the civil law attorney page? A: List all five categories on the GBP landing page. Re-theme the civil law attorney category page to target "car accident attorney" by using that keyword in the title tag, H1 (combined with civil law attorney), and the link from the homepage. Car accident attorney becomes the first service listed under the civil law attorney category.

Q: Should I use our branded 844 number or local GBP number on location pages? A: The phone number on the GBP should match the one on the website's location page. You can have a sitewide branded number in a banner, but the location-specific page must display the GBP phone number.

Q: Should I reply to all 50+ old Google reviews that were never answered? A: Yes, reply to every single review regardless of how old it is. Every review should have a response.

Q: Can I have multiple GBPs registered at the same home address? A: Yes, 2-3 is rarely a problem as long as the primary categories don't significantly overlap, and the phone number, website, and business name are all different. Problems start at 5-10+ listings. Google doesn't state a limit but will deprecate excess listings so they only show for branded searches.



🔮 Emerging Trends & Challenges





AI-generated local map pack results are showing up in 10-15% of local searches. These seem to weight proximity less heavily than the traditional map pack, which could change local SEO strategy over time.



LLM optimization (getting mentioned by ChatGPT, Gemini, etc.) is becoming a distinct service category alongside traditional SEO, with significantly higher price points.



GBP call tracking remains unreliable. Use third-party call tracking numbers if accurate data matters.

Search volume data from third-party tools is unreliable for local SEO. Entity-based research (checking if GBP results differ between keywords) is more useful than traditional keyword volume research.



Tool hopping and over-planning (e.g., setting up a CRM before doing any outreach) remain common productivity traps. Focus on doing the work rather than optimizing the tools.

Seasonal GBP Category Switching for HVAC

A detailed discussion explored how HVAC businesses in northern climates can handle dual seasonal needs (air conditioning vs. heating). Options include switching the primary category seasonally (e.g., air conditioning repair in spring, heating system repair in fall), though this risks triggering GBP re-verification. Caleb's preferred long-term approach: pick one primary category, build topical relevance for both over time, and adjust homepage content seasonally rather than constantly switching categories. When you do switch, update title tags, H1 tags, and images — and switch back before the next season starts, not after.

"Near Me" Rankings vs. City-Name Rankings

"Near me" searches weight proximity much more heavily than city-name searches. This means rank maps for "near me" keywords need to be smaller. Caleb shared a real example: a multi-location veterinary client in a Dallas suburb where a six-mile rank map showed the top competitor at only 11% top-three. Shrinking to a two-mile radius brought the top competitor to 70% — a realistic target to beat. Always size your rank map so the leading competitor falls in the 60–90% top-three range.

Service Area Business: Adding a Physical Address

For a lawn care business in Warsaw, Indiana, Caleb strongly recommended adding a visible address. Even though Google says you shouldn't add an address unless you see clients there, the ranking improvement is massive. Tips: be ready for re-verification (especially video verification), coach your client that the correct answer to Google's phone call is "yes, we sometimes meet clients here," and make sure the business name matches the license and all documentation exactly.

E-Commerce SEO: Swiss Pepper Spray Store

Caleb walked through a live audit of a Swiss e-commerce website that had spent $25K on a previous agency with no results. Difficult Client: Dentist Not Ranking Despite 50+ Pages

A member asked about a dental client in a competitive unincorporated area who isn't cracking the top 20 despite having ~50 pages. Caleb recommended starting with a content audit using Copyscape (checks for external plagiarism), Siteliner (checks for internal duplicate content), and Screaming Frog (reviews title tags, H1s, and overall site structure). Often these stuck clients have low-quality blog content that doesn't build topical relevance. The fix: prune or merge weak content, build out Core 30 pages, and aggressively source local links through chambers of commerce.

AI Optimization (AEO/AIO)

The process to optimize for AI systems like ChatGPT is very similar to traditional SEO. AI systems look for schema, well-organized websites, and quality mentions/citations. Key tactics: monthly Google News press releases, active presence on Reddit, LinkedIn, Medium, and Facebook, and potentially paid placements through services like PRASAP.com. The target is approximately 250 brand mentions across the web. Also critical: make sure the GBP name matches the brand name everywhere, since AI systems aren't as forgiving as Google about name variations.

Google Removing the "Call Now" Button from SERPs

Google has started removing the direct "Call Now" button from local search results. Users now must click into the GBP profile first before seeing the call option. This means GBP profile optimization becomes even more important for driving conversions. Some of Caleb's clients have seen call drop-offs, while others haven't been affected much — largely depending on whether the profile was optimized purely for SEO or also for conversions.

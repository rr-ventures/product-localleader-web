- The session opened with discussion of a proven client acquisition approach: offering a 7-day fast track free trial where you compress a month's worth of ad spend into one week
- No retainer or setup fee is charged during the trial, making it an easy sell compared to competitors who require $1,200-1,500 service fees plus a 3-month minimum commitment

- Clients are willing to spend $900-1,000 in ad spend during the trial week because they avoid the upfront service fees they would pay elsewhere

### Target Market & Pricing Strategy

- Primary focus should be on building companies, specifically granny flat builders and bathroom/kitchen renovation companies, as they have bigger projects and budgets compared to individual trades
- Individual trades like electricians, painters, and tilers can still work, especially since their shorter sales cycles (e.g., painting projects require fewer decisions) can be beneficial
- For the average building company, a base offer price point of $1,500-1,800/month makes sense
- Starting at $999/month is acceptable to get runs on the board and establish cash flow quickly
- Pricing should match the type of client, not just existing social proof - approximately 66% of any market consists of average clients who should be targeted with appropriately priced services
- Moving to $2,000-3,000/month requires targeting bigger businesses with higher revenue

### Base Offer Deliverables

- The core offer should include: Meta ads lead generation with AI chatbot for lead pre-qualification and nurturing
- Additional low-hanging fruit deliverables to include:
    - Google My Business optimization
    - Directory submissions
    - Reputation management campaign with review requests (can use GoHighLevel's built-in tools and incentivize with prize drawings)
- Key principle: "Simplicity scales, complexity fails" - don't overcomplicate the MVP
- Deliverables to avoid in early stages:
    - Full SEO content creation
    - Competitor reports (can be done informally during onboarding calls)
    - Content kits
    - Landing page builds
    - Local SEO pages
- These additional services position the value beyond just paid ads - clients are also building assets through their Google My Business profile and reputation
- The offer should be communicated based on outcomes: amount of project revenue acquired or quote consultations booked monthly

### SEO Considerations

- Full-scale SEO is a separate service that dedicated agencies charge up to $6,000/month for, takes much longer to show results, and should be reserved for whale clients as a significant upsell
- If offering intensive SEO, charge around $2,000/month and subcontract to an SEO specialist
- Basic Google My Business optimization is sufficient for the base offer as it provides free traffic and credibility when potential clients research the business after seeing ads

### Differentiating with Technical Skills

- Reece has strong leverage through extensive tech/IT/AI background and coding skills
- The biggest pain point for building companies is time spent on quoting - some builders spend 50+ hours quoting jobs they might not even win
- Potential differentiation opportunities include:
    - Building automation/AI infrastructure to streamline the quoting process
    - Creating "exotic technology" in the customer journey that provides leads with immediate estimates or automated light quotes based on project scope
    - Implementing done-for-you appointment setting and closing assistance (what some large home services agencies offer)
- Important: Don't build custom solutions until after speaking with real clients to understand their specific needs
- Any AI/automation offering must be clearly defined with tangible workflows that address specific pain points, not ambiguous "free AI builds"

### Market Research Approach

- Before finalizing any offer elements, conduct conversations with real businesses in the target market
- Key question to ask: "When it comes to getting help and partnering with a marketing company, what exactly are you looking for right now and what are the main things you feel like you need help with?"
- Listen to how they describe their needs in their own language and use that language when communicating deliverables
- Example: A mortgage broker agency discovered the clawback pain point (losing commission when clients refinance within 12 months) and built a unique guarantee around it without changing fulfillment
- The insights from appointment setting calls and sales calls provide the highest quality information for making confident offer decisions
- Set up informal conversations with building companies and trades in your network to gather initial insights
- In early stages, maintain flexibility to pitch different things on different sales calls based on what each prospect needs
- Be authentic when testing new deliverables: explain it's not standard but you know how to build it and want to test it with them at no extra charge

- Don't over-intellectualize or feel the offer must be set in stone - it will evolve naturally through client interactions

- You don't need to dramatically reinvent the wheel to get started and build momentum

Summary

### Service Offering Decision

- Discussed transitioning from free test projects to a paid service model
- Explored two main options: paid ads vs. low-ticket SEO/Google Maps services
- Reviewed Alex Hormozi's advice on avoiding the "no man's land" of mid-ticket agency services ($1,500-$3,500/month) due to high churn
- Hormozi recommends either low-ticket ($150-$500/month) with high margins and low churn, or high-ticket ($10k+/month) for larger businesses

### Sales Framework Training

- Reviewed the "three pillars" sales framework for structuring and delivering pitches
- Emphasized customizing the pitch based on client pain points rather than delivering a scripted presentation
- Discussed using client case studies and stories to pre-handle objections for each pillar

### Chosen MVP Offer

- Decided on a low-ticket SEO service targeting home services/trades businesses
- Three pillars: First page Google ranking, Google Maps ranking, and reputation management
- Pricing: $500/month ($125/week) with minimal fulfillment costs
- Service designed to be highly scalable with VAs/contractors at $100 or less per client monthly

### Current Situation

- Currently working with five unpaid test clients (two painters, one electrician, one tiler)
- Successfully achieved first page Google rankings for some clients, though tracking systems not yet implemented
- Concerns about client capacity and stress levels addressed - reminded that scaling and hiring can happen gradually

### Market Opportunity

- Identified gap in market for anti-paid-ads SEO services
- Many trade businesses lack websites, Google My Business profiles, or proper SEO
- Low barrier for ROI - one job typically covers annual service cost
- Service can track tangible metrics like Google My Business calls and profile visits

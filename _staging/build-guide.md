## 1. SITE PURPOSE & CONVERSION OBJECTIVE

The website is a **validation and conversion tool** — not a discovery tool. Visitors arrive via paid ads, cold outreach (email/DM/SMS), or warm referrals. They're already somewhat aware of Local Leader.

**The entire site exists to do two things:**

1. Build enough trust and belief that the prospect takes action.
2. Convert them on a CTA.

**Primary CTA:** "Book a Free Strategy Session" — links to the Google Calendar booking link above. This is the #1 conversion goal. Repeated throughout the site after every major section.

**Secondary CTA:** "Request a Local Ranking Audit" — lower friction entry point. Links to a thank-you page that confirms the request and presents the calendar booking. Feeds into a lead nurture sequence designed to move the prospect toward booking a strategy session.

**CTA rules:**

- One dominant CTA repeated consistently across the site (Book a Free Strategy Session).
- Same CTA after each major section (hero, pillars, pricing, proof).
- Secondary CTA only where it serves a different buyer state (prospect not ready for a call but curious about where they rank).
- Never have more than 2 CTAs competing for attention on any single screen.

## 2. RECOMMENDED PAGE STRUCTURE

Based on competitor research and what converts in this market. The best sites in this category move quickly from promise → proof → contact. No heavy storytelling. No long scrolls before proof appears.

### Section 1: Hero

- **Headline:** Hook (e.g. "Become the #1 tradie in your area.")
- **Subheadline:** Supporting angle + risk reduction (e.g. "We get you to the top of Google Maps, first page of Google, and build your 5-star reputation — no ad spend required.")
- **Primary CTA:** "Book a Free Strategy Session"
- **Secondary CTA (optional):** "See How It Works" or "Request a Local Ranking Audit"
- **Visual:** Google Maps-style card or before/after (see Section 3 below)

### Section 2: Social Proof Strip

Directly after or near the hero. Logos, review count, star ratings, or placeholder stats. Lightweight trust signal before the visitor scrolls into detail.

### Section 3: Problem Section

Short, punchy. "If you're not in the top 3 on Google Maps, you're invisible." Frame the gap between where they are and where they should be. Keep it to 3–5 lines max.

### Section 4: Three Pillars

Outcome-led with a light "how" underneath each. Frame as what the prospect gets, not what we do.

**Pillar 1: Top of Google Maps**

- **Headline:** "Show up first when locals search for your trade."
- **Outcome:** When someone searches "[your trade] near me," Google shows 3 businesses on the map. If you're not in those top 3, you're invisible. We get you there and keep you there.
- **Light how:** Full Google Business Profile optimisation, 50+ directory listings, ongoing Maps ranking management across your whole service area.

**Pillar 2: First Page of Google**

- **Headline:** "Your business on Page 1 — not your competitors, not some directory. You."
- **Outcome:** Organic traffic that comes to you every day without spending a cent on ads.
- **Light how:** SEO-optimised pages targeting the exact words your customers type. Technical fixes. Suburb expansion as rankings grow.

**Pillar 3: 5-Star Review Engine**

- **Headline:** "Build a wall of 5-star reviews — without lifting a finger."
- **Outcome:** More reviews means more trust means more calls. The obvious choice before anyone picks up the phone.
- **Light how:** Automated review requests after every job. Happy customers go to Google. Concerns go to a private feedback form. Every review gets a professional response.

**CTA after this section.**

### Section 5: How It Works

Simple 3-step process. Keep it visual and scannable.

1. **Book a call** — We learn about your business, your area, and your goals.
2. **We build your plan** — We set up and optimise everything across Google Maps, Search, and Reviews.
3. **Your phone starts ringing** — Leads come in on autopilot. You choose the jobs you want.

**CTA after this section.**

### Section 6: Pricing

- **Tier 1 display:** ~~$2,000/month~~ → **$500/month** — EARLY BIRD OFFER
- Framing: Limited spots. Founding client pricing. Transparent about the fact this is a new offer and early clients get a significant discount. You keep that rate as long as you stay.
- Don't list granular deliverables here. Keep it clean. The three pillars sections do the explaining. Pricing section just confirms: "Everything above. One price. No surprises."
- No ad spend required on top. No lock-in after initial 3-month commitment. Month-to-month after that.
- ROI framing near pricing: "What's your average job worth? One extra job from Google more than covers it." / ~$125/week.

- **Tier 2 display:** Minimal. 3–4 lines total. "For businesses ready to take the next step." List capability groups (Brand & Design, Digital Presence, Systems & Automation) without long feature lists. "Custom pricing — book a call to discuss." The authority comes from it existing, not from explaining every line item.

**CTA after this section.**

### Section 7: Proof / Case Studies

Testimonials, before/after data, results. All placeholders for now — design should accommodate real proof being swapped in easily.

**Proof types to design for:**

- Video testimonial placeholders
- Written testimonials with name / trade / location
- Before/after ranking data
- Google review count growth (e.g. "14 → 52 reviews in 4 months")
- Google Maps position improvements
- Stats blocks (calls generated, ranking improvements, review growth)

**Proof placement guidance:**

- Social proof strip directly after or near the hero
- Case study / proof block after the three pillars
- Testimonials near the pricing section
- Proof near every CTA repetition

**CTA after this section.**

### Section 8: About the Founder

Brief. 3–5 sentences. Photo optional. Reece is the guide, the tradie is the hero. Frame around "I built this because tradies deserve better than what's out there." No long origin story.

### Section 9: FAQ / Objections

See objection handling table in the Brand Context artifact. Copy is website-ready. Design as an expandable accordion or similar clean format.

### Section 10: Final CTA Block

Repeat primary CTA with urgency framing. "Limited early bird spots available." This is the last thing they see before the footer.

## 3. HERO VISUAL DIRECTION

The hero visual should reinforce the outcome — being #1 on Google in your area.

**Primary concept:** A Google Maps-style card showing the tradie's business at #1 position in the Map Pack with strong reviews (e.g. 4.9 stars, 100+ reviews). Should feel like looking at an actual client result, not a mockup.

**Before/after variant:** Before: buried on Google, few reviews, not in Maps. After: #1 position, wall of 5-star reviews, the obvious choice. (Reference: Birdeye runs this exact before/after concept in ads and it performs well long-term.)

**The visual should feel like proof — data-forward, not decorative.**

## 4. DESIGN PREFERENCES

These are soft preferences — if paired with a separate design system or template, that system takes priority on visual execution.

- Clean, light, professional aesthetics.
- Typography-led design over heavy imagery.
- Generally prefers left-aligned hero layouts.
- Light background, high-contrast layouts (this is what converts in the tradie category).
- Whitespace that feels confident, not empty.
- **Mobile-first design** — tradies browse on their phones in the ute. This is the primary viewport.

**Dislikes:**

- Dark/moody backgrounds, neon, gradients, startup/tech-bro vibes.
- Excessive animations and scroll effects.
- Heavy assets that slow page load.

---

## 5. TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS

- **Site type:** Single-page or minimal-page site. One long-scroll homepage is fine. Additional pages only if needed (e.g. /pricing, /about, /blog in future).
- **Editability:** All copy should be easily editable — this is version 1 and will evolve as real proof comes in.
- **Componentised testimonials:** Design so placeholders can be swapped to real testimonials without rebuilding.
- **Mobile-first:** Most visitors will be on phones. Design and test for mobile viewport first.
- **Page speed:** Keep it fast. No heavy assets. Tradies on mobile data won't wait.
- **CTA links:** Primary CTA ("Book a Free Strategy Session") links to Google Calendar: `https://calendar.app.google/ceK9b3Jr3KGD9cCs5`. Secondary CTA ("Request a Local Ranking Audit") links to `/v{N}/thank-you` which presents the calendar.
- **Placeholder testimonials:** Label as placeholders in code comments so they're easy to find and replace.

## 6. COMPETITIVE CONTEXT FOR DESIGN DECISIONS

**What the best competitor sites do:**

- Lead with outcome language ("more leads / more jobs").
- Heavy risk-reversal: no lock-in, free audits, guarantees.
- Heavy social proof: review walls, testimonials, badges.
- Simple, low-friction CTAs.
- Plain, tradie-coded language.
- Light background, high-contrast layouts.
- Move quickly from promise → proof → contact.

**Where Local Leader can win:**

- Showing pricing on the website = immediate trust advantage (most competitors hide it).
- Proof that's forensic, not broad — show the work, show the baseline, show the movement.
- Positioning around dominance and asset-building, not just lead flow.
- Out-clarify and out-proof the market, not out-design it.
